Life of the Muscle Car

Muscle Car. The term originiated for the 1960s and early 1970s special editions of cars which were deigned for drag racing. Although frequently debated, muscle cars typically had the following characteristics:

  • V-8 engine
  • Rear wheel drive
  • Affordable
  • Designed for straight-line drag racing
  • Lightweight two-door body

The 1949 Oldsmobile Rocket 88, is cited to be the first full-sized muscle car. It was the first time a V-8 engine was available in a smaller and lighter body style. The Rocket 88 was said to have "launched the modern era of the high-performance V-8."

The muscle car saw it's decline in mid 1970s due to an oil crisis and new laws calling for better fuel efficiency. Many muscle cars were forgotten about and rusted out.

The 1968 Ford Mustang GT may be the most iconic muscle car of all, thanks to its co-starring role in the Steve McQueen movie Bullitt. McQueen piloted a Highland Green Mustang to immortality in what many consider to be the greatest movie car chase of all.
